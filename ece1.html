What is E-Waste?

E-waste refers to discarded electrical or electronic devices. This includes items such as computers, televisions, smartphones, and other gadgets.

Why Proper Disposal Matters

Improper disposal of e-waste can lead to environmental pollution and health hazards due to toxic materials found in electronics. Proper disposal helps protect the environment and conserves resources.

How to Dispose of E-Waste

  1. Identify items for disposal.
  2. Check local regulations and guidelines for e-waste disposal.
  3. Contact local e-waste recycling centers.
  4. Prepare the items for transport, removing batteries if necessary.
  5. Drop off the items at the designated recycling location.

Recycling Centers

Here are some resources to find local e-waste recycling centers:

  • EPA Electronics Donation and Recycling
  • Call2Recycle
  • Earth911

Tips for Disposal

  • Backup your data before disposal.
  • Consider donating functioning devices.
  • Remove personal information from devices.
  • Check for manufacturer take-back programs.
